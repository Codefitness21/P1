Sports Betting

“I won!” …are words one likes to say and hear when betting on a team! I was asked by the marketing side of the company I am employed with, Digital Seat, to create a sports betting app that can be used for casino apps. More specifically, Caesars Sportsbook and Casino requested a mock design of the sports betting module that could potentially be used. Due to my lack of betting experience, I began researching to understand their app layout, how it operates and the way in which bets are made. Although Caesars has a plethora of information currently in their app, my intention was to simplify it and make it easier for non-experienced users such as myself to navigate and understand it with ease. Using myself as the subject of the project, I designed the module based on how I and others would understand it. I researched other sports betting apps such as Drafts Kings, BetMGM, and Action Network Sports Betting and drew comparisons of what I liked, disliked and could use to simplify the app.

Once I understood the direction of my research, I began designing low and high fidelity wireframe mockups in an XD document, outlining and prototyping how I wanted the app to look and operate. Once finalized and approved, I began coding the app using html, css, and javascript designed for mobile view. CSS flexbox was used to structure most of it for purposes of regulating the spacing and dimensions thus allowing proper alignment for images and text. Sections and containers were used to organize the html. A carousel was used to display the different teams and betting options. I used an html table to create the betting section and css grid to design the bet now calculator. Caesars' green and gold colors were used throughout for the text, buttons, and button borders. An abstract background layout was selected from the freepik website, which complemented the colors and overall theme of the app. Sorry, you won't win anything but click the View Project button below and enjoy!!
